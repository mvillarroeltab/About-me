WELL HELLO THERE

I am Marina Villarroel, a creative, architect and aspiring web developer based in London, UK.

I have a passion for creativity, architectural design, and web design. I was born in Salamanca, Spain; but spent my young adulthood in Madrid where I studied Architecture. I migrated over to London in search of opportunities. This is not just a dynamic and amazing city, but also one that gives you so many options and different paths. All of my working life so far has been in London, and although I remain passionate about architecture and problem solving, I feel there is more to explore in the tech industry to fulfill my design focus and creativity. The pandemic has also made me reevaluate things and value flexibility and dynamism, which the construction world lacks. I started my coding world in Udemy and I have caught the coding bug; I would love the opportunity to keep learning with Founders and Coders.

What can I offer.

A background in architectural Design

Currently working as an architect at the architecture and interior design practice TP Bennett in London where I have been involved in the design process of several new builts since conception to delivery. I have eight years of experience in the architecture and design industry, which I plan on bringing to the tech industry. Some of these transferrable skills include defining the problem and analysing the needs and requirements; planning a solution and prototyping; creating an original design from concept to implementation.
I have a keen interest in technological innovation to support the design process and is one of the reasons why I started my coding path by learning to implement the BIM system in the office.

A keen passion for Web Development

As a very design focused person I like to keep it simple. My goals are to focus on typography, content and conveying the message that you want to send. Order and layout is important to me; an understanding of the importance of the white space and a minimalistic approach. As Mies van der Rohe said: 'Less is more'. Accessibility; readability and good functionality is what makes it tick.
