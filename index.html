Marina Villarroel
WELL HELLO THERE

I am Marina Villarroel, a creative, architect and aspiring web developer based in London, UK.

I have a passion for creativity, architectural design, and web design. I was born in Salamanca, Spain; but spent my young adulthood in Madrid where I studied Architecture. I migrated over to London in search of opportunities. This is not just a dynamic and amazing city, but also one that gives you so many options and different paths. All of my working life so far has been in London, and although I remain passionate about architecture and problem solving, I feel there is more to explore in the tech industry to fulfill my design focus and creativity. The pandemic has also made me reevaluate things and value flexibility and dynamism, which the construction world lacks. I started my coding world in Udemy and I have caught the coding bug; I would love the opportunity to keep learning with Founders and Coders.

My Skills.

Architecture Design

Currently working as an architect at the architecture and urban planning international practice Allies and Morrison in London where I have been involved in cutting edge projects such as the new BBC Studios at our budding Stratford East cultural hub where I am currently finalising Stage 4 and a new office development in Canada Water.
Previously I worked for four years at the Cambridge based architectural practise MCW Architects where I was involved as a fully qualified architect in several educational projects.
I have a keen interest in technological innovation to support the design process and is one of the reasons why I became one of the key people to implement the BIM system in MCW. This has given me an in-depth knowledge of Autodesk Revit. Outside of the office, I have been broadening my knowledge, trying out Dynamo and other software.

Web Development

As a very design focused person I like to keep it simple. My goals are to focus on typography, content and conveying the message that you want to send.

My Projects.



A foundation by the coast

The new cultural foundation dedicated to Antonio Coderch will house a library space, documentation centre and archive, and a small museum which function will be to display Antonio Coderch life's works.



A place of learning

The Learning Hub at the new Waterside Campus of the University of Northampton is the central piece that will house under one roof the Library, student administration, shared general teaching areas, specialists’ skills spaces, academic workspaces and catering. Such different spaces are made possible with the use of a central open space as connector. The building has been thought to be accessible 24 hours/day.



Where music happens

The new BBC Studios at Stratford East will provide a new home for the BBC Symphony Orchestra, the BBC Symphony Chorus, the BBC Singers, and BBC Rock & Pop, all currently based in Maida Vale, west London. This multi-purpose facility will be used primarily for rehearsal, recording and broadcast.
